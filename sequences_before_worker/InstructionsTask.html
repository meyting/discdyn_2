Task:

In the first part/ before the main part of the experiment starts, you are asked to answer up to {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} questions consisting of a sequences of numbers within {{C.time_sequence_mins}} minutes. In order to find the missing number of a sequence, you need to figure out the pattern according to which the number sequence is constructed. The task ends either after {{C.time_sequence_mins}} minutes or after you have completed all {{C.NUM_ROUNDS}} sequences.

Example:

9 , 8 , 7 , 6 ,  

In this example, the rule according to which the sequence of numbers is constructed is to subtract 1 from the previous number in the sequence. Therefore, the correct answer would be "5".

You can always skip a sequence by clicking on "Next". In that case you will be directed to the next sequence. Please note that you can never go back to a previous question. Instead of clicking "Next" you can also press "enter" to submit your answer and move on to the next sequence.

Payment:

Your performance in this task determines your bonus payment for part I.

The bonus payment for the task depends on a self-set target number of points. Before the task starts, you are asked to set yourself a target score between 0 and {{C.NUM_ROUNDS}}. You will afterwards receive a bonuspayment depending on the amount of that target score but only if you solve at least as many questions correctly as you have specified before.

On the next pages, you will get more information about the bonus payment and will be asked to specify your self-set "target" score.


On the next page, you can try the task in a short training phase. In this practice round you have {{C.time_sequences_training}} minute to complete {{C.num_sequences_training}} sequences. The result of the training phase does not influence your payment.
